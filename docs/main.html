  • Biznify

    Biznify is a website that helps prospective small business owners find the ideal place to setup shop. This website was implemented by my team of two and I for the annual Inrix Hackathon in just under 24 hours and earned top 6 projects out of 43 competitors. Our app queries data from Datafiniti on retail space available for rent based on a location that the user enters. We then use tranportation data from Inrix to rank the results based on parking availability and trips to a specified location, with the option to add more data in the future if we were to deploy this as a service. I used the Google Maps API to render the front end maps display for the recommended locations.

  • PACRR: Piloted Autonomous Crisis reconnaissance Robot

    The aim of PACRR is to create a low cost, autonomous capable quadruped robot for first responder applications like search and rescue, detecting gas leaks, and other situations where it is dangerous or too confined for humans. We are starting with an open source design for a 3D printed robot dog, then modifying it to be better suited for rugged and unsafe applications. Companies like Boston Dynamics have demonstrated that quadruped robots are not simply novelty but have actual life-saving capabilities in dangerous situations during their partnership with the New York Fire Department. However, these robots are nearly $75,000, which is far out of the budget for smaller fire departments and other disaster relief organizations. Our design will be cheaper, simpler, and more expendable, allowing even small organizations to use it.

    • Raspberry PI
